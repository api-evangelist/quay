The Middle East is transitioning from experimental AI initiatives to production-ready implementation, representing a critical driver of national economic competitiveness and resilience. AI is projected to generate approximately $320 billion in economic value for the Middle East by 2030, making flexible infrastructure strategies essential for capturing this opportunity. This post examines why open, hybrid approaches enable organizations to adapt to rapidly shifting AI workload requirements across the region.
